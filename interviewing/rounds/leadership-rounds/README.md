# Leadership Rounds (CTO · Head of Product · Founder) + Reverse Interview

## What's tested

These rounds test **alignment**, not a fixed syllabus. Each interviewer is checking a distinct question:

- **CTO**: Would I trust this person to make unsupervised technical decisions? Do they have opinions grounded in experience, or just surface familiarity?
- **Head of Product**: Can engineering-me be reasoned with? Do I think in user problems and outcomes, or just in implementations?
- **Founder/CEO**: Do they actually care about this mission? Can they operate under ambiguity and own outcomes at scope?
- **Reverse interview**: Not a courtesy — you are being evaluated on the quality and depth of your questions.

## Format

30–60 min conversation, usually at the final stage. Low structure, high variance. Expect:
- One deep technical-judgment thread (CTO)
- One product-thinking thread (HoP)
- Genuine back-and-forth (not structured Q&A)

The signal they're reading: do you have considered views, do you update on new information, do you ask good questions?

## Per-interviewer angles

### CTO
Prepare opinions with reasons about their likely stack tensions:
- Build-vs-buy decisions (especially for model serving, evals, observability)
- Model vendor strategy and switching costs
- Eval discipline and when AI systems go to production
- Tech-debt honesty — what you'd push back on, not just what you'd fix

### Head of Product
Think in user problems and metrics trees, not feature lists:
- "Should we build feature X?" → interrogate the problem behind it first
- "How do you prioritize?" → outcomes over output, ruthless about what not to build
- Know their recent product bets and have a considered view on them

### Founder/CEO
- Why this mission, specifically — not "AI is interesting" but what about *their* version of it
- 90-day plan that shows learn-then-ship instinct, not hero mode
- Comfort operating at multiple layers (code + process + strategy) simultaneously

## Prep checklist

- [ ] Research their engineering blog, CTO/HoP talks or posts, recent product launches, funding stage
- [ ] Prepare your 90-day sketch: learn → small shippable win → one process improvement offered
- [ ] Rehearse two judgment threads: a technology bet you'd make today, and a hype you'd resist
- [ ] Select your reverse-interview questions per interviewer (not the same list for all three)
- [ ] Decide your real deal-breakers before walking in — so you evaluate honestly, not hopefully
