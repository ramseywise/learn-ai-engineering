# Sources — Leadership Rounds

## Internal cross-references

These rounds are deliberately open-ended — any topic may surface. The reverse interview in particular can open doors to deep dives on technical, product, or operational topics.

| Guide | Relevance to this round |
|-------|------------------------|
| `product-business` | Foundation for HoP conversations: user problems, metrics trees, feature prioritization, market thinking |
| `system-design` | Powers CTO judgment threads: trade-off method, build-vs-buy, architectural reasoning under constraints |
| `evals` | Central to CTO rounds at AI companies: eval maturity, production gates, regression tracking |
| `technical-questions` | Background for any technical depth the CTO pulls on |
| `coding-challenge` | May inform questions about AI-assisted coding practices and code review policy |
| `behavioral` | Overlaps heavily — many behavioral questions surface in leadership rounds, especially around conflict, ownership, and failure |
| `case-study` | Informs the product-thinking thread; HoP conversations often follow a case-study structure |

## External resources

### Reverse interview frameworks
- **reverse-interview GitHub** (github.com/viraptor/reverse-interview) — community-maintained list of questions to ask employers; useful for expanding beyond the question bank here
- **"Questions I'm asking in interviews"** — Joel Spolsky's classic; still relevant for engineering culture signals

### CTO interview prep
- **Lethain's writing** (lethain.com) — Will Larson on engineering leadership, staff+ expectations, and how senior technical leaders evaluate candidates; directly applicable to CTO round prep
- **"The Engineering Executive's Primer"** — Larson's book; chapters on technical strategy and org design map to likely CTO conversation threads
- **CTO Craft** (ctocraft.com) — community for CTOs; their content library covers common CTO interview angles

### Startup due-diligence
- **YC's Series A / B due-diligence checklists** — useful inverse: the questions VCs ask are close to the questions you should ask
- **"Joining a startup checklist"** — Elad Gil's "High Growth Handbook" chapter on due diligence before joining; covers runway, cap table, retention signals
- **Levels.fyi company reviews** — compensation and culture data; useful baseline for whether what you're hearing matches what employees report

### AI-specific hiring context
- **"How AI Changes Hiring"** (swyx.io / lmstudio posts) — practical takes on how AI tooling is shifting what senior engineers need to demonstrate
- **Anthropic's model cards and system prompts** — reading actual production AI documentation sharpens your language for talking about eval, safety, and model judgment in interviews

### Product thinking for engineers
- **"Shape Up"** (basecamp.com/shapeup) — Basecamp's product development methodology; gives vocabulary for feature scoping, appetite-based decisions, and what "killing a feature" actually means
- **Shreyas Doshi on product sense** (twitter/substack) — direct applicability to HoP conversations; especially his writing on input vs output metrics and what distinguishes good product thinking
