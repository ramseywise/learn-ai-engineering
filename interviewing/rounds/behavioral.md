# Behavioral Round

## What's tested
Evidence of how you actually operate: collaboration, conflict handling, ownership,
adaptability, self-awareness. Interviewers pattern-match your *specific past behavior*
against their failure modes — vague virtue claims score zero; concrete stories with your
role, your decision, and a real outcome score.

## Format & trends
30–60 min, late in most loops; sometimes split into values/bar-raiser variants (research
F5 — usually not a separate prep target). The consulting/FDE flavor leans on adaptation
stories: a failure you learned from, a client who rejected your recommendation,
disagreeing with a stakeholder (notes: case-interview). 2026 probes add: "tell me about
a time an AI tool misled you" — have one.

## STAR, with the two upgrades
**S**ituation (2 sentences max) → **T**ask (your responsibility) → **A**ction (the bulk —
*I*, not *we*) → **R**esult (a number or observable outcome). Upgrades:
1. **+ Reflection**: end with what you changed going forward — the growth-mindset close
   mirrors what system-design rounds score (adapt, don't defend).
2. **Match the story to the failure mode they're screening for**: startup loops screen
   for ambiguity tolerance and speed; enterprise/FDE loops for stakeholder patience and
   trust-building. Same story bank, different emphasis.

## Prep checklist
- [ ] Story bank of 8–10 STAR stories covering: conflict with a peer, disagreement with
  authority, failure/mistake owned, ambiguous scope rescued, tight deadline triage,
  mentoring/uplifting someone, pushing back on a bad requirement, learning something
  hard fast.
- [ ] Each story: 2-minute spoken version + numbers in the Result. Rehearse aloud.
- [ ] Map stories to the company's values page the night before — reuse the bank,
  re-angle the telling.
- [ ] Prepare the failure story to be a *real* failure (not "I work too hard") with a
  visible behavior change — accept feedback, adjust direction fast is exactly what
  production environments need (notes: case-interview).
- [ ] Nonprofit/mission orgs: prepare a "why this mission" story that's specific, not
  generic do-gooding ([product-business guide](../guides/product-business.md) §4).

## Question bank
- "Tell me about a conflict with a coworker." — technical disagreement, steelman their
  side, resolution via data/prototype, relationship intact after.
- "A time you failed." — real stakes, your causal role stated plainly, the fix, the
  changed behavior since — no hedging.
- "A time you had to deliver with incomplete requirements." — clarify what you could,
  state assumptions explicitly, ship the reversible version, iterate on feedback.
- "Disagree and commit — when did you?" — voiced the objection with reasons, lost,
  committed fully, and either was proven wrong (say so) or revisited with new data.
- "How do you handle feedback you disagree with?" — separate the observation from the
  prescription; test it on evidence; thank-and-adopt or respectfully close the loop.
