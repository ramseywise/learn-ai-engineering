# Behavioral Round

## What's tested
Evidence of how you actually operate: collaboration, conflict handling, ownership,
adaptability, self-awareness. Interviewers pattern-match your *specific past behavior*
against their failure modes — vague virtue claims score zero; concrete stories with your
role, your decision, and a real outcome score.

## Format & trends
30–60 min, late in most loops; sometimes split into values/bar-raiser variants. The consulting/FDE flavor leans on adaptation stories: a failure you learned from, a client who rejected your recommendation, disagreeing with a stakeholder. 2026 probes add: "tell me about a time an AI tool misled you" — have one ready.

## The method (STAR + two upgrades)

**S**ituation (2 sentences max) → **T**ask (your responsibility, explicitly yours) → **A**ction (the bulk — *I*, not *we*) → **R**esult (a number or observable outcome)

### Upgrade 1: Add Reflection
End with what you changed going forward. The growth-mindset close mirrors what system-design rounds score (adapt, don't defend). An answer that ends at Result is a B. An answer that ends with the behavior change is an A.

### Upgrade 2: Match to the failure mode they're screening for
Interviewers probe specific failure modes that burned their team. Startup loops screen for ambiguity tolerance and speed; enterprise/FDE loops for stakeholder patience and trust-building. Same story bank, different emphasis. Map stories to the company's values page the night before — reuse the bank, re-angle the telling.

## Prep checklist
- [ ] Story bank of 8–10 STAR stories covering: conflict with a peer, disagreement with authority, failure/mistake owned, ambiguous scope rescued, tight deadline triage, mentoring/uplifting someone, pushing back on a bad requirement, learning something hard fast, AI tool that misled you.
- [ ] Each story: 2-minute spoken version + numbers in the Result. Rehearse aloud.
- [ ] Map stories to the company's values page the night before — reuse the bank, re-angle the telling.
- [ ] Prepare the failure story to be a *real* failure (not "I work too hard") with a visible behavior change.
- [ ] Nonprofit/mission orgs: prepare a "why this mission" story that's specific, not generic do-gooding.
- [ ] For FDE and consulting roles: prepare 1–2 adaptation stories specifically (client rejected your recommendation, stakeholder who pushed back, ambiguous requirements).
