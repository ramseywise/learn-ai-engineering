# Recruiter / HR Screen

## What's tested
Narrative coherence and mutual fit, not technical depth: can you say who you are, why this
role, and why now in under two minutes — and do logistics (comp, timeline, location,
authorization) rule you out before anyone technical spends an hour on you.

## Format & trends
15–30 min call, usually the first stage of every loop (research F4). The recruiter is
scoring communication and enthusiasm, and pattern-matching your story against the req.
2026 note: recruiters increasingly probe AI-tool fluency directly ("how do you use
LLMs/copilots in your work?") — have a concrete, honest answer.

## Prep checklist
- [ ] 90-second narrative: arc (past → present → this role), one flagship project, why
  this company specifically. Rehearse out loud.
- [ ] One-line versions of your 2–3 headline projects (the deep versions live in
  [project-deep-dive](project-deep-dive.md)).
- [ ] Comp framing ready: a researched range, delivered as "I'm targeting X–Y based on
  the market for this role — is that in band?" Deflect exact-number pressure early;
  never anchor below your floor.
- [ ] Logistics answers pre-decided: notice period, hybrid/remote constraints, timeline,
  other processes in flight (saying "I'm in other loops" is leverage, not rudeness).
- [ ] 2–3 questions for them: team size, loop structure, timeline to decision.

## Question bank
- "Walk me through your background." — the 90-second narrative; end pointing at *this* role.
- "Why are you leaving / why us?" — toward, never away-from; name something specific about
  their product or mission.
- "What are your comp expectations?" — range + "flexible for the right role"; ask their band.
- "What are you looking for in your next role?" — mirror the req honestly (scope, stack,
  stage) — this is a filter question.
- "Tell me about a project you're proud of." — headline project, one-liner + impact number.
